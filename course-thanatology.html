    Program Overview

    Introducing you to the discipline of thanatology. Take a deep dive into the fascinating world of dying, death, and bereavement.

    Accreditation

    The Association for Death Education and Counseling (ADEC) is the leading professional association in thanatology and death education. The Edgewood University Thanatology curriculum is aligned with the ADEC Essential Body of Knowledge.

    Edgewood College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission (HLC).

    Key Highlights
    • Experience a one-of-a-kind curriculum focused on evidence-based content and aligned with the ADEC Essential Body of Knowledge.
    • 10 dynamic electives to choose from.
    • Achieve 30-credit Master's Degree.
    • Learn in 100% asynchronous online format.
    • Over 1300 hours of learning.
    • Student support available all days.
    • Achieve Edgewood Alumni Status.
    Why Pursue a Career in Thanatology?

    A career in thanatology offers roles such as:

    • Bereavement support in hospices or hospitals
    • Funeral directing
    • K-12 education or nursing
    • One-on-one grief support or group facilitation

    While clinical degrees may be needed for therapy, additional education in grief, loss, and trauma is essential. The Master of Science in Thanatology opens doors to meaningful careers in death and grief support.

    Certificate

    Degree Awarded: Master of Science in Thanatology (E-Learning) — from Edgewood University

    Learning Outcomes
    • Develop deeper empathy and understanding for those experiencing death, loss, and grief.
    • Gain skills to provide effective support in hospices, hospitals, and grief counseling settings.
    • Contribute to shaping policies and practices in palliative care, bereavement support, and death education.
    • Learn how to apply the four core competencies of Interprofessional Collaborative Practice to enhance care for families and individuals confronting dying, death, loss, grief, and suicide.
    • Unlock Edgewood College Alumni Status.
    Top Topics You Will Learn
    • Empathy and Compassion
    • Effective Communication
    • Grief Counseling
    • Cultural Competency
    • Critical Thinking
    • Emotional Resilience
    Who Is This Course For?

    Designed for working professionals or individuals interested in providing support to the dying and bereaved. Graduates are prepared for roles in hospices, hospitals, and grief centers.

    Graduation Requirements

    To be awarded the Master of Science in Thanatology (E-Learning):

    • Completion of all coursework.
    • GPA requirement: minimum 3.00 (grades C and above).
    • Assessment based on assignments and projects.
    Minimum Eligibility
    • Minimum Age: 21 years old
    • Academic Level: Bachelor's Degree in any field with minimum GPA 2.75 / 4.0
    • Language Proficiency:
      • Bachelor's where English is the mode of delivery, or
      • IELTS 6.0 or equivalent
    • Other Requirements: Personal essay (500–1,000 words) describing motivation to study thanatology and relevant career goals.
    For Working Professionals
    • 100% Online
    • Fully Online Format
    • Full-Time: 36 Months
    • Recommended Study Time: 12–15 hours per week
